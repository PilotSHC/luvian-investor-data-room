**Executed.** This agreement assigns to Luvian Labs LLC all intellectual property created by the founder in connection with the company, including all pre-formation work that became part of the codebase, designs, and written materials of Luvian.

**Download the executed PDF using the button above.**

## What it covers

| Category | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Source code, designs, and prototypes built before formation | Assigned to the company on a "from inception of the project" basis |
| Source code and designs built after formation | Assigned to the company in the ordinary course |
| Inventions, improvements, and discoveries during the engagement | Assigned to the company |
| Trademarks, brand assets, and goodwill in the "Luvian" mark | Assigned to the company |
| Confidentiality | Founder is bound by confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations covering all proprietary information |

## Why investors look at this first

IP ownership is the single most-reviewed item in pre-seed diligence. A founder who has not assigned pre-formation work to the company creates an immediate diligence flag at the Series A: the LP and acquirer want unambiguous ownership of every artifact in the codebase. We executed this agreement at formation to remove that risk before it can compound.

## Coverage going forward

Every additional contributor, co-founder, employee, contractor, or advisor, signs an equivalent Confidential Information and Invention Assignment (CIIA) before contributing to the codebase or design system. The status of each is tracked in the *IP Assignment Index* document elsewhere in this section.

## Effect after the Delaware Flip

On the LLC → Delaware C-corp Reorganization, all assignments granted to Luvian Labs LLC pass to the successor Delaware corporation by operation of law (statutory conversion) or by the merger or asset-transfer mechanic of the Reorganization. No re-assignment is required; the chain of title is preserved.
