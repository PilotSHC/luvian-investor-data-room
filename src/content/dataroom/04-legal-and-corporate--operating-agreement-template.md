---# Operating Agreement — Luvian Labs LLC (Single-Member)

> **DRAFT TEMPLATE.** Single-member LLC operating agreement. Most US states do not legally require a single-member LLC to have an Operating Agreement (state-default LLC act fills in the gaps), but it is **strongly recommended** for: (i) preserving limited liability and the corporate veil, (ii) opening business banking, (iii) demonstrating to investors and counsel that the LLC is properly formed, and (iv) authorizing actions like issuing convertible notes. This is a starting draft — review with counsel before signing. See `_LEGAL_DISCLAIMER.md`.

---

## SINGLE-MEMBER OPERATING AGREEMENT OF LUVIAN LABS LLC

**A [STATE — confirm DE / WY / CA / etc.] Limited Liability Company**

This Operating Agreement (this "**Agreement**") of **Luvian Labs LLC** (the "**Company**") is adopted as of **[FORMATION_DATE_OR_EXECUTION_DATE]** by and between the Company and **Stephan Claxton** (the "**Member**").

### Recitals

The Company was formed as a limited liability company under the laws of the State of **[STATE]** by the filing of a Certificate of Formation (or Articles of Organization, as applicable) with the **[STATE]** Secretary of State on **[FORMATION_DATE]**. The Member is the sole member of the Company. The parties enter into this Agreement to set forth the terms governing the Company's operation.

### 1. Formation and Name

(a) **Name.** The name of the Company is **Luvian Labs LLC**. The Company may also conduct business under such fictitious or assumed names as the Member determines.
(b) **Principal Office.** The Company's principal office is located at **[COMPANY_ADDRESS]**, or such other location as the Member determines.
(c) **Registered Agent.** The Company's registered agent and office are as set forth in its Certificate of Formation, as amended.
(d) **Term.** The Company has perpetual duration and will continue until dissolved as provided in Section 9.

### 2. Purpose and Powers

(a) **Purpose.** The Company is organized for the purpose of designing, developing, marketing, and selling browser-native, AI-native systems engineering software, and any other lawful business purpose.
(b) **Powers.** The Company has all powers permitted under the laws of the State of **[STATE]**.

### 3. Member; Membership Interest

(a) **Sole Member.** **Stephan Claxton** is the sole Member of the Company, and owns **100%** of the membership interest in the Company.
(b) **No Certificates.** Membership interests will not be evidenced by certificates unless the Member elects otherwise.
(c) **Admission of Additional Members.** Additional members may be admitted only with the written consent of the Member, by amendment to this Agreement, and on such economic and governance terms as the Member determines.

### 4. Capital Contributions

(a) **Initial Contribution.** The Member has contributed (or will contribute on or before the date hereof) cash, intellectual property, services, and other property to the Company in exchange for the Member's membership interest, as recorded in the Company's books and records.
(b) **Additional Contributions.** The Member is not required to make additional capital contributions but may do so at any time. Additional contributions are recorded in the Company's books and records.
(c) **No Interest.** No interest is paid on capital contributions.
(d) **Capital Account.** A capital account will be maintained for the Member in accordance with applicable Treasury Regulations.

### 5. Distributions; Allocations

(a) **Distributions.** Distributions of cash or other property are made to the Member at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, subject to applicable law and any restrictions in financing documents.
(b) **Allocations.** All items of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit are allocated to the Member, who is treated as the owner of all such items for U.S. federal and state income-tax purposes.
(c) **Tax Treatment.** The Company will be treated as a **disregarded entity** for U.S. federal income-tax purposes for so long as it has a single member, unless the Member elects otherwise.

### 6. Management

(a) **Member-Managed.** The Company is managed by its Member. The Member has full authority to act on behalf of and bind the Company.
(b) **Officers (Optional).** The Member may from time to time appoint officers (e.g., President, CEO, CTO, CFO, Secretary), each of whom serves at the Member's discretion. Initial officers, if any, are listed in **Schedule A**.
(c) **Authority to Bind.** Any action taken by the Member or by an authorized officer in the name of the Company binds the Company.
(d) **Compensation.** The Member is entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Company, as determined by the Member.
(e) **Authorization for Convertible-Note Issuance.** The Member, on behalf of the Company, is hereby specifically authorized to (i) negotiate and execute Convertible Promissory Notes, Note Purchase Agreements, and Pro-Rata Side Letters substantially in the forms in `../05_Agreements_and_Contracts/`, (ii) deposit the proceeds in the Company's bank accounts, and (iii) take all such further actions as may be necessary or convenient to consummate any such financing.

### 7. Books, Records, and Reports

(a) **Books and Records.** The Company will maintain books and records in accordance with U.S. GAAP or such other basis of accounting as the Member determines.
(b) **Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company is the calendar year, unless the Member determines otherwise.
(c) **Tax Returns.** The Company will file or cause to be filed all required federal, state, and local tax returns. Because the Company is a disregarded entity, items of income, gain, loss, and deduction will flow through to the Member's personal tax returns (Schedule C, Form 1040) unless and until an election is made to be treated as a corporation for tax purposes.

### 8. Indemnification

The Company will, to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, indemnify and hold harmless the Member, any officers, and any other persons designated by the Member from any liability, loss, damage, or expense (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of any claim relating to the business of the Company, except in cases of willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breach of fiduciary duty.

### 9. Dissolution and Winding Up

(a) **Events of Dissolution.** The Company will dissolve upon (i) the written election of the Member, (ii) the entry of a decree of judicial dissolution, or (iii) any other event mandating dissolution under applicable law that is not otherwise cured.
(b) **Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's assets will be applied first to creditors (including the Member as a creditor, if applicable), then to the Member as a return of capital, and finally to the Member as a distribution of remaining assets.
(c) **Reorganization.** The Member may at any time reorganize the Company as a corporation (including a Delaware C-corp), including by merger, conversion, or asset transfer. Such a Reorganization is **not** a dissolution of the Company under this Section 9.

### 10. Conversion to Corporation (Anticipatory)

The Member acknowledges that the Company intends to convert into a Delaware C-corporation (Luvian, Inc.) prior to or in connection with a Qualified Financing (as defined in any Convertible Promissory Note). Such conversion may be effected through a statutory conversion, a merger into a newly-formed corporation, or such other means as the Member determines (the "**Reorganization**"). Upon the Reorganization, this Agreement terminates and is replaced by the Bylaws and Certificate of Incorporation of the resulting corporation, as amended from time to time.

### 11. General Provisions

(a) **Governing Law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of **[STATE]**, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles.
(b) **Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended at any time by the written consent of the Member.
(c) **Severability.** If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in effect.
(d) **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement, together with the Company's Certificate of Formation, constitutes the entire agreement of the Member regarding the operation of the Company.
(e) **Counterparts; Electronic Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature.

## Drafting Notes (delete before signing)

- **State of formation matters.** Delaware and Wyoming are most common for tech startups; California has higher franchise taxes. Confirm with counsel and have this template re-keyed.
- **Operating Agreement vs. Bylaws.** This is the LLC equivalent of corporate Bylaws. It is replaced at the Reorganization by `Bylaws_TEMPLATE.md` (already in this room).
- **Tax treatment.** Single-member LLCs default to "disregarded entity" status for federal income tax (income flows to Stephan's Schedule C). Most pre-seed founders keep this default. Election to be taxed as an S-corp can save self-employment tax once revenue and salary exceed certain thresholds — defer until counsel/CPA recommends.
- **Bank account.** Banks (Mercury, Brex, Chase) require this Operating Agreement to open a business account in the LLC's name. Have it executed before opening the account.
- **No 83(b) needed at LLC stage** for the founder's membership interest. 83(b) elections become relevant if (a) profits-interest grants are made to others, or (b) at the Reorganization when the Member's membership interest converts to founder common stock subject to vesting.
- After execution: file in `../_private/Corporate_Documents/Operating_Agreement_<execution_date>.pdf`, update `Corporate_Documents_INDEX.md`, and bring a copy to your bank.
