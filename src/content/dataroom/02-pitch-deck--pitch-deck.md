## Speaker Notes (selected)

### Slide 2. The Problem

> *"95% of enterprise GenAI pilots produce zero measurable ROI despite $30–40B invested. The failure mode is not that models are bad. It is that engineering context is fragmented, stale, and permission-locked across files, repos, and teams."*
> paraphrased from MIT NANDA, *State of AI in Business 2025*

Gartner's forward look agrees: 40%+ of agentic AI projects will be cancelled by end of 2027 for the same root cause. The category Luvian is building solves the upstream problem.

### Slide 3. Why Now

Four converging forcing functions, each individually credible, together unmistakable.

1. **95% GenAI failure.** MIT NANDA, July 2025. The substrate is broken; AI cannot reason without typed context.
2. **$13.8B in 2025 robotics funding** (vs. $7.8B in 2024). Each marquee round (Figure $39B, Skild $14B, Physical Intelligence $5.6B, Saronic $4B, Shield AI $5.3B, Anduril $61B) is a buyer or partner for an engineering intelligence layer.
3. **DoD MOSA mandate.** December 2024 memo directs MOSA compliance at every SETR, Gate Review, and Program Review for MDAPs. FY2026 weapons portfolio: $384.3B.
4. **IL5/IL6 air-gap as architecture.** No NAT, no DNS to external, no outbound. SaaS context AI (Notion AI, Hebbia, most Glean tenancies) is structurally locked out. This is Luvian's right-to-win.

### Slide 4. The Solution

Four pillars of the context layer for engineering intelligence:

| Pillar | What it does | What it replaces |
|---|---|---|
| Context Acquisition | Connectors into PLM, ALM, simulation, code, requirements, tests, telemetry. | Manual import scripts, brittle middleware. |
| Semantic Normalization | Typed engineering ontology with stable IDs, versioning, and permission boundaries. | "Pile of disconnected strings" that defeats every RAG pipeline. |
| Context Orchestration | Graph traversal, contradiction detection, suspect-link propagation. | Beyond-RAG: not chunked retrieval, structured reasoning. |
| AI Reasoning | Local-first inference. Gap detection. What's missing, what changed, what's at risk. | LLM-wrapper "co-pilots" that suggest from text alone. |

### Slide 6. Differentiators

Luvian's structural advantages, mapped to the patentable innovations the round funds:

| Power | How Luvian wins |
|---|---|
| Counter-positioning | Browser-native plus real-time collaboration is strictly worse for incumbents' existing customer base. They cannot copy without cannibalising seat revenue. |
| Switching costs | Traceability graph and organisational intelligence accumulate per customer. Year-one wedges (evidence, requirements) hand over to year-three locks (modeling, safety + V&V). |
| Process power | The decision graph makes AI better with every interaction. AI makes the graph more valuable with every reasoning loop. Flywheel. |
| Cornered resource | Founding-team domain credibility (autonomy, MBSE, defense delivery). Air-gap deployment posture is engineered in, not retrofitted. |
| Network economies | Real-time collaboration leads to multi-user, then team adoption, then organisation adoption. Cross-artifact unification cannot be matched by point tools. |

### Slide 7. The Wedge

We do not ask buyers to commit to a four-quadrant replacement on day one. The four-phase wedge lands light, expands deep:

1. **Phase 1. Evidence Layer.** Import from Cameo and DOORS, visualise the traceability graph, produce gap-analysis reports. Low switching cost. Days to value.
2. **Phase 2. Requirements.** Author requirements directly with INCOSE A–F grading. Replaces DOORS. Medium switching cost.
3. **Phase 3. Modeling.** SysML v2 visual modeling plus real-time collaboration. Replaces Cameo. High switching cost.
4. **Phase 4. Safety and V&V.** Full decision graph with HARA / TARA / STPA / SOTIF / FMEA plus verification evidence attached. Replaces spreadsheets and Visio. Very high switching cost.

### Slide 9. Competition

The peer set under the new framing is not the legacy MBSE incumbents. It is the cohort that anchors investor expectations for typed, AI-native infrastructure.

| Cohort | Examples | Luvian's structural advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Horizontal context AI | Glean, Hebbia | Glean closed half the on-prem moat via Dell AI Factory. IL5/IL6 + typed engineering ontology remains an open lane. |
| Vertical AI for industrial / autonomous | Cognite, Applied Intuition, Anduril | None of them carries a typed engineering ontology with cross-artifact unification across requirements, models, and safety. |
| Defense Lattice / autonomy stacks | Anduril, Shield AI, Saronic, Foretellix | Adjacent buyers. Potential customers, not category competitors. Anduril Lattice is a tasking bus, not a context graph. |
| MBSE / ALM incumbents | Cameo, Rhapsody, DOORS, Capella, Aras | Wedge displacement targets. Cameo shipped SysML v2 in December 2025; the "first SysML v2 modeler" moat is gone, but cross-artifact unification + AI-native architecture is not. |

Three contestants meaningfully affect the SOM build:

- **Flow Engineering** ($23M Sequoia Series A, October 2025). Same buyer persona. Will compress sales cycles for the wedge if it scales.
- **Trace.Space** (verbatim positioning match, agentic requirements + central knowledge graph). Differentiation collapses to depth of typed ontology + air-gap + safety coverage.
- **Aras Innovator + InnovatorEdge AI**. Graph-based PLM with enterprise distribution and AI overlay; missing SysML v2 + safety methods. Displacement target rather than head-to-head competitor.
