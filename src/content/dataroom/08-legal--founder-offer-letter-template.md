**[YYYY-MM-DD]**

[FOUNDER_NAME]
[ADDRESS]

Dear [FIRST_NAME],

We are pleased to offer you employment with **Luvian, Inc.** (the "**Company**") on the terms set forth below.

## 1. Position

Your title will be **[TITLE — e.g. Chief Executive Officer / Chief Technology Officer / etc.]**, reporting to **[the Board of Directors / the CEO]**. You will perform such duties as are customarily associated with the position, plus other duties as may be assigned by the [Board / CEO]. You will be expected to devote your full business time and effort to the Company.

## 2. Start Date

Your employment will begin on **[START_DATE]** (or earlier if mutually agreed).

## 3. Base Compensation

(a) **Salary.** Your annualized base salary will be **$[AMOUNT]**, paid in accordance with the Company's standard payroll practices, less applicable withholdings. *(For pre-funding founders, this is often $0 or a nominal figure such as $50,000 to $80,000; market-rate compensation typically takes effect at first priced round.)*

(b) **Bonus.** You will be eligible for a discretionary annual bonus targeted at **[X]%** of base salary, subject to performance and Board approval.

## 4. Equity

You have already been issued **[NUMBER]** shares of the Company's Common Stock pursuant to the Restricted Stock Purchase Agreement dated **[GRANT_DATE]** between you and the Company. Such shares vest as set forth in that Agreement (4 years monthly with a 1-year cliff; double-trigger acceleration).

This offer letter does not modify or supersede the RSPA.

## 5. Benefits

You will be eligible to participate in the Company's standard benefit plans as in effect from time to time, which currently include:
- Health, dental, and vision insurance.
- 401(k) plan **[when adopted]**.
- Vacation policy: unlimited PTO subject to coverage and Board norms.
- Federal holidays.

## 6. Confidential Information and Invention Assignment

As a condition of your employment, you must execute the Company's Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement in the form attached as **Exhibit A** (`CIIA_TEMPLATE.md`).

## 7. Background Check

This offer is contingent on completion of standard reference and background checks, satisfactory to the Company.

## 8. Right to Work

This offer is contingent on your providing documentation establishing your identity and authorization to work in the United States within three business days of your start date, as required by federal law (Form I-9).

## 9. At-Will Employment

Your employment with the Company is **at will**, which means that either you or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause and with or without notice. This at-will status may not be changed except by a written agreement signed by you and the [CEO / Board].

## 10. Severance and Acceleration

Severance and equity-acceleration entitlements (if any) on termination are set forth in the RSPA and any separately executed agreements (such as an indemnification agreement). Beyond those, no severance is provided.

## 11. Confidentiality of Offer

You agree to keep the terms of this offer confidential, except as required by law or shared with your immediate family or legal/financial advisors who are themselves bound by confidentiality.

## 12. Entire Agreement

This offer letter, together with the RSPA, the CIIA, and any duly authorized indemnification agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between you and the Company concerning your employment. It supersedes any prior representations or agreements, whether written or oral.

## 13. Governing Law

This offer letter is governed by the laws of the State of **[Delaware / California]**.

If you accept these terms, please sign and return this letter together with the executed CIIA. We are excited to have you on the team.

Sincerely,

**[NAME]**, **[TITLE — typically Chair or another board member]**
For and on behalf of **Luvian, Inc.**

## Drafting Notes (delete before sending)

- For pre-funding founders staying equity-only: substitute "$0" for the base salary or omit the offer letter entirely until first priced round (founders are then often W-2 to support state-tax filing and 401(k)/payroll).
- The offer letter does **not** issue equity — equity is issued via the separate RSPA.
- 401(k), health benefits, and similar plans are typically not in place at pre-seed; bracket out or remove if not applicable.
- Confirm with counsel whether state-specific notices are required (e.g. NY wage-theft prevention act notice, MA personnel-records statute).
