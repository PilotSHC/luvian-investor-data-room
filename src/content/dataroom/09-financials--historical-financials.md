## What this is

A three-year forward pro forma for Luvian Labs LLC, covering the
window from pre-seed close through the back half of the Series A
deployment cycle. It is intentionally lean. Pre-seed companies do not
forecast revenue from contracts they have not signed. Instead, this
sheet shows:

1. The shape of capital deployment across the round and the Series A.
2. The headcount ramp tied to milestone progress.
3. The customer and ARR trajectory anchored to the *Market Eval v2*
   SOM build (15 to 40 strategic accounts at $750K to $2M ACV in
   defense, aerospace, and tier-1 auto and robotics).
4. The cash position year over year at lean, base, and stretch cases.

The detailed by-month build is in the editable model. This document
is the narrative summary investors evaluate first.

## What this is not

- A five-year line-itemised "enterprise" model. At pre-seed that is
  theatre.
- A revenue commitment. Pre-seed revenue is *bonus*, not *budget*.
- A precision exercise. Where a single number appears it represents
  the midpoint of a credible range.

## Key assumptions

1. **Pre-Seed close in 2026-Q3** at $1.75M base, $1.5M lean, $2.0M stretch.
2. **Three priority hires in Year 1**: GTM Lead (month 2), Founding
   ML and AI Engineer (month 4), second Software Engineer (month 6).
3. **First paid pilot** in the $150K to $500K range, six-month term,
   signs around month 9 from pre-seed close. Treated as bonus
   runway, not budgeted into base case revenue.
4. **Series A close** at month 18 to 24 post pre-seed close, $10M
   base, priced into the post-Flip Delaware C-corp.
5. **ACV ladder**: $150K to $500K design-partner pilots → $750K to
   $1.5M strategic-account first contracts → $1M to $2.5M
   renewal-and-expand by Year 3.
6. **Gross margin** trends from ~70% (custom-deployment-heavy early)
   to ~80% by Year 3 as on-prem packaging and the multi-tenant
   cloud profile harden.
7. **No paid acquisition.** Demand-gen is reputation-led and
   architecture-memo-led. The GTM motion is founder-led plus the GTM
   Lead, engineering-leader-to-engineering-leader.

> EBITDA-adjusted breakeven late in the year; the upper sensitivity
> band crosses earlier.

## Sensitivity (Year 3 outcomes)

| Case | Strategic accounts | Year 3 ARR exit | Year 3 net loss / (EBITDA) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Lean** | 12 | $8M | ($1.5M) |
| **Base** | 16 | $12M | ~$0 |
| **Stretch** | 20 | $20M | +$2M (positive) |

The biggest single driver of variance is **time-to-first-paid-
deployment**. Compressing the first paid contract from month 9 to
month 6 (with Lead-investor recruiting help on the GTM Lead and the
founding ML engineer) shifts Year 3 outcomes meaningfully toward the
stretch case.

Secondary drivers, in declining order: (1) Series A timing, (2)
foundation-model API price decline, (3) the first strategic-account
expansion contract converting at the upper end of the ACV ladder.

## Round economics

**Pre-Seed:** $1.5M – $2.0M Convertible Note. Base case $1.75M. No
interest. No discount. Cap negotiated with the Lead.

**Use of funds (base case):**

- 55% engineering hires and existing-team payroll
- 15% design-partner conversion
- 10% AI, cloud, and infrastructure
- 8% on-prem packaging
- 5% legal and compliance
- 7% operations and buffer

The detailed quarterly burn shape and the salary policy are in *Use
of Funds* and *Financial Forecast*. The full editable model is in
the diligence-tier *Financial Model Summary*.
