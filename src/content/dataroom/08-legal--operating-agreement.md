## Why this matters at the LLC stage

Single-member LLCs are not legally *required* to have an Operating Agreement in most US states, the state-default LLC act fills in the gaps. We adopted one anyway because it is what investors, banks, and counsel expect to see, and because it materially helps in four places:

1. **Limited liability.** It documents the corporate veil between Stephan as a person and Luvian Labs as an entity.
2. **Banking and treasury.** Mercury and similar startup-friendly banks want a current operating agreement before opening business accounts.
3. **Investor diligence.** Counsel for any incoming Convertible Note investor will ask for it on the first call.
4. **Authorisation.** It explicitly authorises the Member to issue Convertible Notes and execute the planned LLC → Delaware C-corp Reorganization without needing further consent.

## Effect after the Delaware Flip

On execution of the planned LLC → Delaware C-corp Reorganization, this agreement is superseded by the Certificate of Incorporation, Bylaws, and Initial Board Consent of the successor Delaware corporation. The Convertible Notes auto-convert into preferred stock at that point under their own conversion terms.
