## 1. Purpose

The purpose of the **Luvian, Inc. 20[YY] Equity Incentive Plan** (the "**Plan**") is to attract, retain, and motivate employees, consultants, and directors of **Luvian, Inc.** (the "**Company**") and its subsidiaries by offering them the opportunity to acquire an equity interest in the Company.

## 2. Types of Awards

The Plan permits the grant of:
- **Incentive Stock Options (ISOs)** — within the meaning of Section 422 of the Internal Revenue Code (Code) — to employees only.
- **Nonstatutory Stock Options (NSOs)** — to employees, consultants, and directors.
- **Restricted Stock** — direct issuance of common stock subject to vesting and repurchase.
- **Restricted Stock Units (RSUs)** — promises to deliver common stock subject to vesting.
- **Stock Appreciation Rights (SARs)** — rarely used at the seed stage.

## 3. Shares Subject to the Plan

(a) **Reserve.** The maximum number of shares of common stock issuable under the Plan is **[450,000]** shares, subject to adjustment for stock splits, dividends, and similar capitalization changes.

(b) **Reuse.** Shares subject to awards that expire, are forfeited, or are otherwise terminated without issuance shall be added back to the available reserve.

(c) **ISO Limit.** No more than the maximum number permitted by Section 422 of the Code may be issued as ISOs.

## 4. Administration

(a) **Plan Administrator.** The Plan shall be administered by the Board of Directors or a Committee designated by the Board (the "**Administrator**").

(b) **Authority.** Subject to the Plan, the Administrator has the authority to:
- Determine eligibility, the number of shares subject to each award, the exercise price (if any), the vesting schedule, and other terms.
- Interpret the Plan and award agreements.
- Make rules of procedure.
- Amend or terminate awards (subject to participant consent for material adverse changes).

## 5. Eligibility

ISOs may be granted only to employees of the Company or its subsidiaries. NSOs, Restricted Stock, RSUs, and SARs may be granted to employees, consultants, and directors.

## 6. Stock Options

(a) **Exercise Price.**
- ISOs: must be at least **100% of fair market value** ("FMV") on the grant date, or **110% of FMV** for 10%-or-greater stockholders.
- NSOs: must be at least **100% of FMV** on the grant date.
- FMV is determined by the Administrator in good faith, typically supported by a **409A valuation** by a qualified independent third party (e.g. Carta, Aranca, Andersen).

(b) **Term.** The maximum term of any option is **10 years** (5 years for ISOs granted to 10%-or-greater stockholders).

(c) **Vesting.** Standard vesting is **4 years with a 1-year cliff** (25% on the one-year anniversary, then monthly thereafter). The Administrator may approve different schedules.

(d) **Post-Termination Exercise.** Vested options must generally be exercised within:
- **3 months** following termination without cause for ISOs (longer windows convert ISOs to NSOs).
- **12 months** following termination due to death or disability.
- The Administrator may extend the post-termination exercise period for NSOs.

(e) **Method of Exercise.** Cash, wire transfer, check, or — at the Administrator's discretion — net exercise, broker-assisted exercise, or stock-for-stock exercise.

(f) **Early Exercise.** The Administrator may, but is not required to, allow early exercise of unvested options, subject to the Company's right of repurchase on unvested shares. Each early exerciser must file an 83(b) election within 30 days.

## 7. Restricted Stock and RSUs

(a) **Issuance Price.** Restricted Stock may be issued for cash, services, or other consideration at the discretion of the Administrator.

(b) **Vesting and Repurchase.** Restricted Stock and RSUs are subject to vesting and to the Company's right of repurchase or forfeiture on termination of service.

(c) **83(b) Election.** Recipients of Restricted Stock are strongly advised to file an 83(b) election within 30 days of grant. (Not applicable to RSUs.)

## 8. Adjustment for Capitalization Changes

The Administrator shall make appropriate, equitable adjustments to outstanding awards (and to the share reserve) for stock splits, stock dividends, recapitalizations, and similar events.

## 9. Change of Control

Unless otherwise specified in the award agreement, the Administrator may, in connection with a Change of Control:
- Provide for **assumption or substitution** of awards by the successor entity.
- **Accelerate** vesting in whole or in part (single-trigger or double-trigger).
- **Cancel** awards in exchange for cash equal to the difference between the per-share consideration and the per-share exercise price (with no payment for underwater options).

## 10. Transferability

Awards are generally non-transferable except by will or the laws of intestate succession. The Administrator may permit transfers to immediate family or estate-planning trusts, subject to the same restrictions.

## 11. Securities Law Compliance

All awards are subject to compliance with applicable securities laws. The Company will rely on Rule 701, Regulation D, or another applicable exemption for grants under the Plan.

## 12. Tax Withholding

The Company has the right to withhold from any award (or to require the participant to remit) any required tax withholding.

## 13. Term of the Plan

The Plan becomes effective on the date adopted by the Board (the "**Effective Date**") and continues until the earliest of:
- 10 years after the Effective Date.
- The date the Plan is terminated by the Board.
- The date no shares remain available for issuance.

ISOs may not be granted after 10 years from the Effective Date or stockholder approval, whichever is earlier.

## 14. Stockholder Approval

The Plan must be approved by the stockholders of the Company within **12 months** of Board adoption. Failure to obtain stockholder approval will result in any granted ISOs being treated as NSOs.

## 15. Amendment and Termination

The Board may amend or terminate the Plan at any time, subject to:
- Stockholder approval where required by law or to maintain ISO status.
- Participant consent for amendments that materially impair existing awards.

## 16. Governing Law

The Plan is governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.
