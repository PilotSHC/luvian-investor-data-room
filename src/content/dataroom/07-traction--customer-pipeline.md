## Stage Definitions

| Stage | Definition | Exit criteria |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Awareness | Person has heard of Luvian (LinkedIn, demo video, intro from network) | First reply or warm intro |
| 2. Discovery | First call held; pain points mapped to Luvian capabilities | Agreement to a deeper technical session |
| 3. Technical eval | Live demo, sample project loaded, technical Q&A complete | Verbal alignment to pilot |
| 4. Pilot scoping | Scope, success metrics, timeline drafted | Signed pilot agreement (LOI) |
| 5. Active pilot | Live deployment in customer environment | Pilot success metrics met → conversion |

## Target Account Profile (TAP)

We are explicitly _not_ chasing every MBSE shop. The TAP for design partners:

- **Industry:** automotive ASIL programs (priority), aerospace/defense, or
  industrial automation with safety-critical workflows.
- **Size:** 50–500 systems engineers; large enough to feel the 5 gripes,
  small enough to move fast.
- **Tooling status:** currently on Cameo or Capella, with active SysML v2
  migration mandate.
- **Champion identified:** a staff or principal systems engineer who has
  publicly criticized current tooling (LinkedIn posts, conference talks,
  forum threads) — easy to source.
- **Procurement:** willing to do a 90-day pilot with a $25K–$50K paid
  engagement, convertible to annual contract.

## Conversion Thesis

We do not believe pre-seed companies should sell broadly. Our motion:

1. **Bring in a design-partner cohort of 3 paid pilots in 2026-Q3/Q4.**
   Stephan owns sourcing via his Applied Intuition network + targeted
   LinkedIn outbound to known practitioners.
2. **Use those pilots to harden on-prem packaging, ASIL workflow polish,
   and the multi-persona surface.** Colin owns the deployment side.
3. **Convert at least 2 of 3 to annual contracts in Q1 2027** ($100K+ ACV
   each) — that's the Series A milestone.
4. **Scale outbound only after one named reference customer is live and
   willing to be quoted.**
