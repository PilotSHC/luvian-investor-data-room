> **DRAFT TEMPLATE.** Standalone side letter granting an investor pro-rata participation rights in the next priced equity financing. Standard for $250K+ check sizes; uncommon for sub-$250K. Based on YC and Cooley GO standard pro-rata side letters. **Review with counsel.** See `../04_Legal_and_Corporate/_LEGAL_DISCLAIMER.md`.

---

Re: Pro-Rata Side Letter — Luvian, Inc.

Dear [INVESTOR_NAME]:

This **Pro-Rata Side Letter** ("**Letter**") is delivered in connection with the **Simple Agreement for Future Equity** dated **[YYYY-MM-DD]**, between **Luvian, Inc.** (the "**Company**") and **[INVESTOR_NAME]** ("**Investor**"), in the principal amount of **$[PURCHASE_AMOUNT]** (the "**SAFE**"). Capitalized terms not defined herein have the meaning set forth in the SAFE.

### 1. Pro-Rata Rights

(a) The Company hereby grants Investor the right (the "**Pro-Rata Right**") to participate in the **next bona fide equity financing** of the Company (the "**Next Equity Financing**") on the same terms as other investors in such financing, up to an amount equal to Investor's **pro-rata share**.

(b) Investor's "**pro-rata share**" is defined as the percentage equal to:
- the number of shares (on an as-converted-to-Common-Stock basis) of Capital Stock held by Investor immediately prior to the Next Equity Financing (after giving effect to the conversion of this SAFE), **divided by**
- the total outstanding Capital Stock of the Company immediately prior to the Next Equity Financing (on a fully diluted basis).

### 2. Notice and Exercise

(a) The Company will give Investor written notice of the Next Equity Financing not less than **15 calendar days** before the closing, including the principal terms (price per share, total raise, lead investor).

(b) Investor must elect to exercise the Pro-Rata Right by written notice to the Company within **10 calendar days** after the Company's notice. If Investor does not respond within that period, the Pro-Rata Right will be deemed waived for that financing.

(c) Failure to exercise the Pro-Rata Right in any one financing does **not** waive the right for any subsequent financing.

### 3. Termination

The Pro-Rata Right shall terminate immediately upon the earliest of:
(a) The closing of an Initial Public Offering of the Company's Common Stock under the Securities Act of 1933.
(b) The consummation of a Change of Control of the Company.
(c) The dissolution of the Company.
(d) Investor's transfer of the SAFE (or the Capital Stock issued upon conversion) to a non-affiliated third party.

### 4. Transferability

This Letter and the Pro-Rata Right are personal to Investor and may not be transferred or assigned without the prior written consent of the Company, except to an affiliate of Investor.

### 5. General Provisions

(a) **Governing Law.** This Letter is governed by the laws of the State of **[Delaware / California]**, without regard to its conflict-of-laws provisions.

(b) **Entire Agreement; Amendment.** This Letter, together with the SAFE, constitutes the entire agreement of the parties on this subject matter and supersedes any prior understandings. This Letter may be amended only by a writing signed by both parties.

(c) **Counterparts; Electronic Signature.** This Letter may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature.

## Drafting Notes (delete before signing)

- Issue **only to investors with check sizes ≥ $250K** (or to operator angels you specifically want long-term involvement from). Issuing to every angel creates a coordination headache at the next round.
- The "next equity financing" definition can be tightened to a "Qualified Financing" with a minimum size (e.g. $5M) to avoid triggering pro-rata on small bridge rounds.
- Pro-rata is a major-investor right — confirm whether this Letter survives only the conversion of the SAFE or extends to subsequent rounds. Some Letters cap pro-rata at the next two rounds.
- Lead investors at Series A often demand the cancellation of pre-existing pro-rata letters as a condition of leading; have counsel ensure the termination provisions in Section 3 do not unintentionally lock out the lead.
