## INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR AGREEMENT

This **Independent Contractor Agreement** (this "**Agreement**") is entered into as of **[YYYY-MM-DD]** (the "**Effective Date**") between **Luvian, Inc.**, a Delaware corporation (the "**Company**"), and **[CONTRACTOR_NAME]** ("**Contractor**").

### 1. Services

(a) **Scope of Work.** Contractor will perform the services described in the **Statement of Work** attached as **Exhibit A** (the "**Services**"). Each Statement of Work signed by the parties is incorporated into this Agreement.

(b) **Performance Standard.** Contractor will perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards.

(c) **Means and Manner.** Contractor controls the means and manner of performing the Services. The Company may direct the desired result but does not control how Contractor delivers it.

### 2. Compensation

(a) **Fees.** The Company will pay Contractor as set forth in the Statement of Work — typically an hourly rate, a fixed-fee deliverable, or a monthly retainer.

(b) **Invoicing.** Contractor will invoice monthly. Undisputed invoices are payable **Net-30** by ACH or wire.

(c) **Expenses.** The Company reimburses pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses, on receipt of supporting documentation.

(d) **Equity.** Any equity compensation is granted under the Company's Equity Incentive Plan and a separate award agreement.

### 3. Independent Contractor Status

(a) **Not an Employee.** Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of the Company. Contractor is not entitled to employee benefits, including health insurance, retirement, paid time off, or workers' compensation.

(b) **Taxes.** Contractor is responsible for all federal, state, local, and foreign income, self-employment, and other taxes on Contractor's compensation. Contractor will provide a completed IRS Form W-9 (or W-8 series for non-U.S. contractors) before the first payment.

(c) **No Authority.** Contractor has no authority to bind the Company without the Company's prior written consent.

### 4. Confidential Information and Inventions

Contractor's confidentiality and IP-assignment obligations are set forth in the **Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement** between Contractor and the Company, executed concurrently with this Agreement (the "**CIIA**"). The CIIA is incorporated by reference and survives termination of this Agreement.

### 5. Representations and Warranties

Contractor represents and warrants that:
(a) Contractor has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement.
(b) The Services will not infringe the intellectual property or other rights of any third party.
(c) Contractor will comply with all applicable laws in performing the Services.
(d) Contractor is not subject to any agreement that conflicts with this Agreement.

### 6. Term and Termination

(a) **Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

(b) **Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement (or any Statement of Work) at any time on **15 days' written notice**.

(c) **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 10 days of notice.

(d) **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Contractor will cease all Services, return all Company property and Confidential Information, and deliver all in-progress work product. The Company will pay for Services performed through the termination date.

(e) **Survival.** Sections 4 (CIIA), 7 (Indemnification), 8 (Limitation of Liability), and 9 (General) survive termination.

### 7. Indemnification

Contractor will indemnify and hold harmless the Company from any third-party claim arising from (a) Contractor's breach of this Agreement, (b) Contractor's negligence or willful misconduct, or (c) any claim that Contractor is or should be classified as an employee of the Company for any tax, benefits, or labor-law purpose.

### 8. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR CONTRACTOR'S INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS UNDER SECTION 7 AND BREACHES OF THE CIIA, EACH PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IS LIMITED TO THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE TO CONTRACTOR IN THE 12 MONTHS PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO LIABILITY. NEITHER PARTY IS LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES.

### 9. General

(a) **Governing Law.** Delaware law (or California, by negotiation), excluding conflict-of-laws rules.

(b) **Dispute Resolution.** *(Optional — strike if not desired.)* Disputes will be resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS in **[San Francisco, California]**, except either party may seek injunctive relief in court.

(c) **Entire Agreement; Amendment.** This Agreement, together with the CIIA and any Statement of Work, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties on this subject matter.

(d) **Assignment.** Contractor may not assign or subcontract without the Company's prior written consent.

(e) **Counterparts; Electronic Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature.

## Drafting Notes (delete before signing)

- **Misclassification risk.** Treating a worker as a contractor when they should be a W-2 employee is a major US tax/labor liability. Apply the IRS 20-factor test (or California ABC test) before defaulting to "contractor." If the worker (i) works set hours, (ii) takes day-to-day direction, (iii) cannot work for other clients, and (iv) uses Company equipment, they are likely an employee.
- **California AB-5.** California has very strict contractor rules. For California-resident contractors, document independent business operation (LLC, multiple clients, set rates, independent equipment).
- **Always pair with the CIIA** — without it, the Company does not own the IP the contractor produces.
- For non-US contractors, confirm tax-treaty status, withholding requirements, and local labor law (especially in the EU and UK).
- For advisors who are not delivering code, a simpler "Advisor Agreement" is more appropriate (typically equity-only, capped at 0.1–0.5% over 2 years).
- File executed copies in `_private/Legal/Employment/` (gitignored). Update `../04_Legal_and_Corporate/Employment_Agreements_INDEX.md`.
