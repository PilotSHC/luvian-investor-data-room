## ARTICLE I — OFFICES

**Section 1.1 Registered Office.** The registered office of Luvian, Inc. (the "Corporation") in Delaware shall be as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation.

**Section 1.2 Other Offices.** The Corporation may have other offices, within or outside Delaware, as the Board of Directors may from time to time determine.

## ARTICLE II — MEETINGS OF STOCKHOLDERS

**Section 2.1 Annual Meeting.** An annual meeting of stockholders shall be held each year on a date and time determined by the Board for the election of directors and the transaction of other business. Failure to hold an annual meeting shall not affect the validity of any corporate action.

**Section 2.2 Special Meetings.** Special meetings of stockholders may be called by (a) the Board, (b) the chairperson, (c) the chief executive officer, or (d) the holders of at least 25% of the outstanding voting stock.

**Section 2.3 Notice.** Written notice stating the place, date, time, and (in the case of a special meeting) purpose shall be given not less than 10 nor more than 60 days before the meeting to each stockholder of record entitled to vote.

**Section 2.4 Quorum.** A majority of the outstanding shares entitled to vote, present in person or represented by proxy, shall constitute a quorum.

**Section 2.5 Voting.** Except as otherwise required by law, the Certificate of Incorporation, or these Bylaws, action by the stockholders shall require the affirmative vote of a majority of the votes cast.

**Section 2.6 Action Without a Meeting.** Any action required or permitted to be taken by stockholders may be taken without a meeting by written consent signed by holders of outstanding stock having not less than the minimum number of votes that would be necessary to authorize or take such action at a meeting at which all shares entitled to vote thereon were present and voted.

## ARTICLE III — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

**Section 3.1 Powers.** The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board.

**Section 3.2 Number.** The number of directors shall be fixed from time to time by resolution of the Board. The initial number of directors shall be **[NUMBER — typically 1, 2, or 3 at incorporation]**.

**Section 3.3 Election; Term.** Each director shall hold office until the next annual meeting of stockholders or until his or her successor is duly elected and qualified, or until his or her earlier death, resignation, or removal.

**Section 3.4 Resignation; Removal; Vacancies.**
(a) Any director may resign at any time upon written notice to the Corporation.
(b) Any director may be removed, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of the holders of a majority of the outstanding voting stock.
(c) Vacancies shall be filled by the affirmative vote of a majority of the directors then in office, even though less than a quorum, or by the stockholders.

**Section 3.5 Meetings.** Regular meetings of the Board may be held at such times and places as the Board determines. Special meetings may be called by the chairperson, the chief executive officer, or any director, with at least 24 hours' notice.

**Section 3.6 Quorum.** A majority of the total number of directors then in office shall constitute a quorum. The act of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present shall be the act of the Board.

**Section 3.7 Action Without a Meeting.** Any action required or permitted to be taken at a Board meeting may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing or by electronic transmission.

**Section 3.8 Telephonic / Electronic Meetings.** Directors may participate in any meeting of the Board by means of conference telephone or other communications equipment by which all participants can hear each other; participation in such a meeting constitutes presence in person at the meeting.

**Section 3.9 Compensation.** Directors shall not receive cash compensation for service as a director unless approved by the Board. Directors shall be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in connection with attending meetings.

## ARTICLE IV — COMMITTEES

The Board may, by resolution adopted by a majority of the directors then in office, designate one or more committees, each consisting of one or more directors, with such authority as the Board prescribes (subject to the limits of the DGCL). Standing committees may include an Audit Committee, a Compensation Committee, and a Nominating Committee, none of which is required at the pre-seed stage.

## ARTICLE V — OFFICERS

**Section 5.1 Officers.** The officers of the Corporation shall be a Chief Executive Officer ("CEO"), a Secretary, and a Treasurer, and may include a President, one or more Vice Presidents, a Chief Financial Officer, and such other officers as the Board may designate.

**Section 5.2 Election.** Officers shall be elected by the Board and shall hold office at the pleasure of the Board.

**Section 5.3 Duties.**
(a) **CEO.** Subject to the direction of the Board, has general supervision over the business of the Corporation.
(b) **Secretary.** Records all proceedings of stockholder and Board meetings; maintains the corporate records.
(c) **Treasurer / CFO.** Has charge of the Corporation's funds and securities.

**Section 5.4 Removal; Resignation; Vacancies.** Any officer may be removed by the Board with or without cause. Any officer may resign at any time on written notice. Vacancies are filled by the Board.

## ARTICLE VI — STOCK

**Section 6.1 Stock Certificates.** Stock may be uncertificated. If certificated, certificates shall be signed by the CEO or the President, and the Secretary or an Assistant Secretary.

**Section 6.2 Transfer.** Transfers of stock shall be recorded on the books of the Corporation upon surrender of the old certificate (if any), endorsement, and compliance with applicable transfer restrictions.

**Section 6.3 Lost Certificates.** The Corporation may issue replacement certificates upon receipt of an affidavit of loss and, if requested, a bond.

**Section 6.4 Record Date.** The Board may fix a record date for the purpose of determining stockholders entitled to notice or to vote at a meeting, or to receive any dividend or distribution.

## ARTICLE VII — INDEMNIFICATION

The Corporation shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless any director or officer to the fullest extent permitted by the DGCL, as set forth in the Certificate of Incorporation. The Corporation may, by Board resolution, extend indemnification to employees and agents on the same basis. Indemnification expenses shall be advanced upon receipt of an undertaking from the indemnitee to repay if ultimately found not entitled to indemnification.

## ARTICLE VIII — GENERAL PROVISIONS

**Section 8.1 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Corporation shall be the calendar year, unless otherwise determined by the Board.

**Section 8.2 Seal.** The Corporation may, but is not required to, have a corporate seal.

**Section 8.3 Books and Records.** The Corporation shall maintain its books and records as required by the DGCL.

## ARTICLE IX — AMENDMENTS

These Bylaws may be amended, altered, or repealed by:
(a) The Board, by a majority vote of directors then in office; or
(b) The stockholders, by a majority of the votes cast.
