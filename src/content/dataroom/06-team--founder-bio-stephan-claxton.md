## Why this founder for this category

The bottleneck for the operational context layer for engineering
organisations is **engineering-organisation literacy**, not
engineering-ability in the abstract. Specifically: the ability to hold
in one person's head how a SysML allocation in Cameo relates to a
Polarion requirement, to a DOORS trace, to a Capella functional chain,
to a simulation result, to a certification artefact under DO-178C / ISO
26262 / IEC 62304.

That is a **5–10 year skill across multiple regulated domains**. It is
not a skill an AI-first founder picks up in a quarter. The category
will be defined by founders who have lived this **on the engineering
side** — the side where the methodology is real, the certification is
real, the cost of being wrong is real — and who also have the
AI / architecture fluency to build the substrate.

Stephan's career sits exactly at that intersection: **MBSE practice in
space and defense at Lockheed Martin**, then **defense systems
engineering and TPM work at Strategic Technology Consulting (Arcfield)**,
then **systems architecture and verification for autonomous vehicles
at Applied Intuition** — three domains that share the same hard
operational core (high-consequence software that has to be right) but
that almost no individual systems engineer has worked in all three of.

That cross-domain fluency is the founder–market fit thesis.

## Career — reverse chronological

### Applied Intuition · Systems Engineer

Systems architecture & verification for autonomous vehicle development.

**Why this matters for Luvian:** Applied Intuition is the contemporary
case study in *being the substrate underneath a regulated-industry
engineering workflow* (autonomy development). The verification +
validation problems for AV stacks are the **prototype problem domain**
for the operational context layer Luvian is now building — connected
artefacts, ontology-grounded reasoning, provenance, suspect-link
propagation across simulation / scenario / requirements / verification
workflows. The lessons of that GTM, that architecture, and that
customer profile are directly applicable to Luvian's wedge customers.

> *"Building autonomy is the hardest verification problem in engineering
> today, and the one rewriting how systems engineering is practiced."*

That is the conviction behind the substrate, lived on the engineering
side first.

### Strategic Technology Consulting (Arcfield) · Sr. Principal Systems Engineer / TPM

Senior systems engineering and technical program management on **defense
programs**.

**Why this matters for Luvian:** the defense wedge in Luvian's
target-customer profile is real, not theoretical. Stephan has worked
inside the **defense-program operational reality** — the regulatory
posture, the security posture, the multi-stakeholder TPM dynamics, the
on-prem / air-gapped deployment expectations, the artefact rigor that
defense primes and integrators expect. This is the customer *and* the
deployment posture Luvian is building for from day one.

### Lockheed Martin · Sr. Systems Engineer

**Model-Based Systems Engineering** for space and defense programs.
SysML modeling, requirements architecture, MBSE practice.

**Why this matters for Luvian:** this is where the **MBSE-practice
depth** comes from. Not "MBSE in theory" but MBSE as it is *actually
practised* inside large engineering organisations — the cultural
reality of how methodology gets adopted, how requirements traceability
is wired across multiple tool stacks, where it breaks down, and what
the operational pain feels like to the engineers living inside it.
This is the foundational layer of credibility the Luvian thesis rests
on. The substrate Luvian is building is what an engineer with this
background *knows is missing* from the existing tool stack.

## Architectural & systems-thinking opinions

These are surfaced verbatim from Stephan's writing at
[stephanclaxton.com](https://www.stephanclaxton.com/) — they are the
philosophical posture behind the architecture decisions across this
data room:

- **"Structure produces behavior."** *The architecture of a system is
  its actual design. Change the architecture and you change what the
  system does. Change anything else, and you mostly change the story
  you tell about it.* — This is the pre-conviction behind every
  architecture decision in Luvian's substrate. The substrate **is** the
  product.
- **"Requirements traceability is institutional memory. It feels useful
  exactly once, on the worst day. That is enough."** — The conviction
  behind the provenance + suspect-link primitives.
- **"The aerospace tradition gave systems engineering its methods. AI
  and autonomy are giving it back its relevance, and making executable
  models the new default."** — The Why-Now thesis condensed to one
  sentence: the convergence of decades of aerospace SE methodology
  with the AI-native moment is the window Luvian is building into.
